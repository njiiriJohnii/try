√ History:

1. Establishment

Nyangwa Boys School was established in 1965 on Harambee basis on 50 acres piece of land and became a Government Assisted Single Stream in 1967 to 1978. The school acquired Double Stream status from 1979 to 1983. Towards the end of 1983, Nyangwa Boys School was registered as a Three Streamed School, the status it maintained until July 2007. From 2008, the school was registered as a Four Streamed School. Currently, in the new devolved government, within the new constitution, the school is a Four Streamed Public County Extra Boys Boarding School

2. Status

  1. Centre of excellence for Mbeere South
  2. Four streamed county – extra boys boarding school
  3. Current enrolment – 740

3. Staffing

  1. Teaching staff of 31 TSC teachers, 8 BOG teachers and 1 on Teaching Practice
  2. The school has an approved CBE of 35 teachers hence it is understaffed by 4 teachers
  3. Support Staff – 33 members
